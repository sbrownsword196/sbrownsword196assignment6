The Story of Molly Jacobson and Bee Campus USA

A Mission Across America

From the rolling hills of the Hudson Valley to the coastal charm of Martha's Vineyard, pollinator ecologist Molly Jacobson travels with a critical message. Her lectures draw packed audiences, but the real work happens back home in Syracuse, New York, where she meticulously studies the bees, butterflies, wasps, and moths that keep our ecosystems alive.

Jacobson's entire career has been dedicated to solving one of our most pressing environmental crises: the massive decline in pollinator populations that threatens both biodiversity and food security worldwide.

The Bee Campus Revolution

In April 2022, SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry became an official Bee Campus USA affiliate, with Jacobson leading the charge as co-chair. This national initiative represents a growing movement among universities and cities to create sanctuaries for native pollinators.

The Student Connection

Brooke Shaw, a Vermont native with a deep love for nature, discovered Jacobson's work through an environmental journalism class. After learning about the challenges facing native pollinators, Shaw was inspired to join the team as a field technician.

"I was so impressed, enthralled really, with her work," Shaw recalls. Her role extends beyond data collection‚Äîshe becomes an educator whenever students stop to observe the campus pollinator gardens, sharing knowledge about the critical importance of these tiny creatures.
